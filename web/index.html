    Key Differences: GTD Pro vs GTD

    Parameter GTD Pro GTD
    Purpose / Driver Level Professional class: only all-pro driver lineups Pro-Am class: mixed lineups with professional and amateur drivers
    Driver Ratings All drivers FIA Platinum/Gold Combination of Pro (Platinum/Gold) + Silver/Bronze
    Cars GT3-spec cars, same models as GTD GT3-spec cars (identical technical specifications)
    BoP Applied Applied (same as GTD Pro)
    Race Strategy / Pace Aggressive strategy, full pro stints Often more conservative strategy, with restrictions for amateur drivers
    Series / Events IMSA WeatherTech: full season including Rolex 24, Sebring, Long Beach IMSA WeatherTech: full season, but with driver rating restrictions
    Teams Usually factory-backed professional teams Often customer/privateer teams and amateur drivers

    Parameter GTD / GTD Pro
    Class TypeGT3-spec cars
    ChampionshipsIMSA WeatherTech GTD / GTD Pro
    ChassisFIA-homologated GT3 chassis (steel or aluminium spaceframe / carbon monocoque depending on manufacturer)
    BodyworkManufacturer-specific GT3 aero kit, homologated by FIA
    Engine TypeFIA-homologated GT3 engines (turbocharged or naturally aspirated, production-based)
    Engine DisplacementManufacturer-specific, within FIA GT3 limits (~2.0–4.0 L)
    Maximum Power~500–600 hp (regulated via BoP)
    Hybrid SystemNot permitted
    DrivetrainRear-wheel drive (standard GT3); some AWD permitted in BoP rules
    Minimum WeightDetermined by BoP; typically 1,245–1,360 kg depending on model
    FuelFIA-approved renewable fuel; series-specific fuel flow regulated
    GearboxSequential, 6–7 speed; spec or homologated per GT3 rules
    TyresSingle supplier per championship (Michelin / Continental / Pirelli)
    AerodynamicsHomologated GT3 aero kit; cannot be modified outside FIA update rules
    BoPMandatory; regulates weight, power, fuel flow, restrictor size
    Driver LineupGTD Pro: all-pro drivers (Platinum/Gold FIA); GTD: Pro-Am mix (Platinum/Gold + Silver/Bronze)
    Pit / Driver RegulationsGTD Pro: professional pit stops, flexible stint length; GTD: restrictions on amateur driving time per stint
    DimensionsMax width ~2,000 mm, length ~4,900–5,000 mm, height ~1,200 mm; per FIA GT3 homologation
    NotesGTD Pro and GTD use the same cars; main difference is driver lineup and strategic restrictions. BoP ensures parity across all GT3 models.
